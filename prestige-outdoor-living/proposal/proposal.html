Performance & Search Ranking

Speed is a Competitive Advantage — and a Google Ranking Factor

In 2021, Google officially made Core Web Vitals (Largest Contentful Paint, Cumulative Layout Shift, Interaction to Next Paint) a direct ranking signal. WordPress sites with plugins routinely underperform on these metrics. The redesign is engineered from the ground up to score ≥ 90 on Lighthouse.

7%

Revenue Loss Per Second of Delay

Amazon found a 1-second page load delay results in a 7% loss in conversions. For a pool company converting 2 leads/month at $60k, one slow second costs ~$100k/year.

Source: Amazon internal study, cited by Akamai Technologies
53%

Mobile Abandonment Rate

53% of mobile visitors abandon a site that takes longer than 3 seconds to load. The redesign defers all non-critical scripts and lazy-loads all images below the fold.

Source: Google DoubleClick — The Need for Mobile Speed
Top 3

Google Local Pack Visibility

LocalBusiness JSON-LD structured data (implemented in v4) directly supports eligibility for Google's Local Pack — the map-pinned top-3 results that capture 44% of local search clicks.

Source: BrightLocal — Local Search Consumer Survey

Technical implementation: The redesign uses fetchpriority="high" on the hero image, explicit width and height on every image element (preventing Cumulative Layout Shift), preconnect hints for Google Fonts, defer on all third-party scripts, and CSS transform + opacity-only animations (which run on the GPU compositor thread and never trigger layout recalculations). These are not aesthetic choices — they are measurable Lighthouse score improvements.

Gallery & Portfolio

"Show, Don't Tell" — The Highest-Confidence Signal in Premium Services

When a customer is deciding whether to spend $60,000 on a pool, words like "expert team" and "proven track record" are table stakes. What actually moves a prospect to pick up the phone is seeing exactly what their backyard could look like. The redesign replaces a basic image grid with a curated, categorized portfolio experience.

  • ✕ Old: Basic WordPress gallery grid with no filtering, no categories, no lightbox. Images may render at inconsistent sizes.
  • ✓ New: 34 high-resolution photos across 5 categories: Pool Installation, Backyard Landscapes, Water Features, Lighting, and Outdoor Kitchens.
  • ✕ Old: Visitor must navigate to separate sub-pages to find specific types of work (e.g. "Lighting" is a separate URL with separate load).
  • ✓ New: Single-page filtering with instant tab switching — no reload. Visitor stays immersed. Live item count updates dynamically.
  • ✕ Old: Clicking an image does nothing — or opens a new page at a smaller size.
  • ✓ New: Full-screen lightbox with prev/next navigation, keyboard arrow support, and touch swipe for mobile. Visitors can examine finished work in detail.
  • ✕ Old: Gallery section not accessible from the main landing page hero or CTA flow — buried in navigation.
  • ✓ New: "Explore Our Work" CTA on SEH reveal links directly to gallery.html. Gallery also accessible via desktop nav and floating mobile bar.

Why this matters for high-ticket sales: A pool installation is a highly visual, emotionally-driven decision. Research by the Nielsen Norman Group shows that for high-consideration purchases, visual evidence of past work is the single strongest trust signal — ranked above testimonials, certifications, and years of experience. The gallery is not a nice-to-have; it is the primary sales tool.

Price Anchoring on Pricing Cards

The "MOST POPULAR" badge on the $69,999 card uses a proven anchoring technique. By presenting the higher option as the default choice, the lower price ($59,999) feels more accessible. Studies show anchoring increases average order value by 15–30% in service industries.

Source: Journal of Consumer Research — Anchoring Effects in Price Perception

Anxiety Reduction Copy

"No pressure, no commitment. Just a conversation about your dream pool." This single line, placed beside the contact form, directly addresses the #1 barrier to form submission in home improvement verticals: fear of a pushy sales call.

Source: CXL Institute — Friction Audit in High-Ticket Home Services

Color Selection = Pre-Commitment

The interactive color swatches (Pure, Nature, Mineral collections) create a micro-commitment before the visitor reaches the contact form. Users who make small interactive choices are significantly more likely to complete a subsequent larger action (form submission).

Source: Cialdini — Commitment and Consistency Principle; applied in CRO by ConversionXL

Mobile Floating CTA Bar

A permanently-visible bottom bar with "Call Now" and "Book Meeting" on mobile eliminates the friction of having to scroll back to a CTA. In mobile UX research, persistent CTAs increase tap-through rates by up to 45% on service industry sites.

Source: Baymard Institute — Mobile UX Research (2024)
